Our Programs

STEMchats’ programs are divided into the three categories of Celebrate, Stimulate, and Innovate. Our for students, by students programs are uniquely designed to impact all students, especially those from disadvantaged backgrounds, through making use of the peer-to-peer technique. Because of this, we are able to provide all of our students a transformative STEM education.

STEMlights

A bi-weekly email based newsletter that highlights upcoming opportunities (i.e. summer programs, research internships, STEM scholarships etc), scientific breakthroughs, and updates about STEMchats’ many programs. A few things that will be in each edition of the newsletter include a "STEM Spotlight" of a teenager making big moves in the STEM field, a short video showing something groundbreaking scientific phenomenons, and at least 3 summaries of STEM breakthroughs. Ultimately, the goal of STEMlights is to provide teens with important STEM information in a creative and interesting way.

IJOYIS

With the acronym representing the International Journal of Youth in Science, this program aims to be the premier scientific journal for that showcases the highest levels of pre-collegiate research from high school students around the globe. A double-blind review process ensures that all segments of submissions are equally represented , to help accommodate for the fact that some students may not have access to high-quality labs or other resources to leverage. Finalists selected for publication to the journal are invited to get involved with internships and volunteer opportunities at scientific research institutions.

STEMchats Institute

This program conducts science curriculums and STEM-related career workshops at public middle schools in Chicago, IL, and San Francisco, California with other volunteer STEMchats members at these sites. The program will be a six-weeks long urban STEM themed workshop series titled “What’s in a City” that aims to cultivate curiosity in the STEM fields. Workshops would be taught using a peer-to-peer technique at underserved schools in order to demonstrate the positive and transformative applications of STEM through hands-on activities, engaged discussion, and real time feedback.

United Engineers

This program conducts design thinking and engineering-related career workshops at public middle schools in Philadelphia, PA and New York City, NY with other volunteer STEMchats members at these sites. The program will be a six-weeks long workshop series based on three of the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals that aims to cultivate curiosity in the STEM fields. Workshops would be taught using a peer-to-peer technique at underserved schools in order to demonstrate the positive and transformative applications of STEM through hands-on activities, engaged discussion, and real time feedback.

i-STEM Scholars

A research program that will connect underprivileged high school students with local professors to perform high-quality, pre-collegiate research. Selected students will be provided with additional mentorship and funding through STEMchats. This program will target these communities and expose students from underrepresented backgrounds to what the STEM field offers. The program hopes to directly impact students by not only offering lab positions but also the ability for them to prepare for college and beyond by having mentors to offer advice that will prepare them for higher education as well as their careers.
